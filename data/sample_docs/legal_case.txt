Legal Precedents and Case Law:
In the landmark case of TechCorp v. InnovateInc (2023), the Supreme Court ruled that algorithmic trade secrets are protected under the Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA).
The court established a three-part test for determining "reasonable measures" of secrecy.
Justice Miller wrote the majority opinion, emphasizing the role of non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) in maintaining trade secret status.
The ruling heavily cited the precedent from Smith v. Jones (1998) regarding the definition of misappropriation.
Counsel for TechCorp argued that unauthorized access to the source code constituted a breach of fiduciary duty.
The permanent injunction was granted, preventing InnovateInc from using the disputed code for five years.
